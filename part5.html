The Turning Point: Finding Redemption Through Faith

On December 25, 2014, I made a life-altering decision to embrace salvation. My pastor gifted me a Bible, and with that, I embarked on a new journey. For many years, I had tried to shape my life my own way, placing my faith in myself, witchdoctors, education, and meticulous planning as the pathways to happiness and success. I had launched numerous projects, each one ending in profound frustration.

During this time, I had distanced myself from church and religion, believing that there was no room for God's intervention in my life. It became a harrowing tale of despair, rejection, unemployment, and depression. I felt utterly alone, harboring a self-loathing that gnawed at my very being. I saw myself as a complete failure.

I vividly recall one fateful afternoon in 2009 when I found myself standing on Nyali Bridge, my head hanging low, burdened by an immense weight of despair. A metaphorical stone hung from my neck. The pain became unbearable, and in my hopelessness, I found myself on that expansive bridge, secretly contemplating a desperate act of consolation. However, while I teetered on the precipice of despair, deep in thought and tears, someone tapped my shoulder and called my name.

Fast forward to my time in Mariakani in 2014. Pastor Jackson found me one morning, sitting idly on a stone by the road leading to his church. I had just taken the final puff from a cigarette I had saved from the previous night's drinking binge. Pastor Jackson introduced a topic I had long avoided: Salvation. He spoke to me about a God who could hear my cries and rescue me from my predicament.

"Behold, the Lord's hand is not shortened, that it cannot save; neither his ear heavy, that it cannot hear: But your iniquities have separated between you and your God..."
— Isaiah 59:1-2

On this day, Pastor Jackson's words struck a chord deep within me. He invited me to accompany him to his church, and I agreed. God had sent him to me at just the right time. At the church, Pastor Jackson shared his own past, revealing how he, too, had lived a life of sin before finding God.

With the guidance of Pastor Jackson, I came to understand that my suffering was a result of my stubbornness. To end this cycle of despair, I had to turn to God and seek His guidance for a new direction. Scripture became my compass. I meditated on Jeremiah 6:16, Zechariah 1:3, and 2 Chronicles 7:14. These verses convinced me that I needed God to break free.

The Bridge to Redemption - Burning Down the Past

Once I had made the pivotal decision to change my life, I visualized myself standing at the precipice of a river, a river that symbolized my current state of poverty and despair. On the other side lay a lush, green land of prosperity. I summoned every ounce of determination and swiftly crossed that makeshift bridge... And then, I did something bold—I burned down the bridge.

With unwavering resolve, I decided to quit smoking cigarettes and bhang, cease alcohol consumption, and abandon miraa chewing all at once. In one day, I left behind the substances that had enslaved me for years. I prayed fervently to God, declaring that I no longer needed these destructive habits. God heard my plea and answered me, removing addiction from my life and filling me with His word.

Isaiah 14:3 resonated with me: "And it shall come to pass in that day that the LORD shall give you rest from your sorrow, and from your fear, and from the hard bondage in which you were made to serve."

My decision to break free from the chains of addiction astonished my friends in the house of despair. They whispered among themselves, skeptical of my resolve. Some even tried to tempt me by bringing beer, but I remained resolute. I had burnt the bridge behind me, leaving no room for retreat.

Through my unwavering decision and the application of scriptural teachings, I have had the privilege of transforming many others. My journey from despair to redemption became a beacon of hope for those who, like me, once found themselves lost in the darkness.
