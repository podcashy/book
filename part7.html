Turn To God

Recalling and recounting the depth of my losses is not something that brings me any joy. Nevertheless, I made a solemn commitment to God, a promise forged in the crucible of my own suffering. I pledged that if He were to rescue me from the abyss, I would utilize my own painful journey as a platform to help others. God, in His infinite mercy, heard my cry.

Much like the Biblical Jonah, I initially resisted God's call. But there comes a point when one must heed the unmistakable instructions of the Almighty. I firmly believe that I have a purpose, and it is now my responsibility to fulfill it for the glory of God.

I urge anyone who hears my story to embrace godliness. God's faithfulness knows no bounds, and He alone possesses the power to reform and transform lives. Regardless of your current circumstances, He will bring healing and guide you towards significant personal growth.

For those who may feel lost, I counsel you to return to the drawing board and contemplate your path (Jeremiah 6:16). God's plans for you are inherently good (Jeremiah 29:11), and by turning to Him, you unlock a future filled with hope and fulfillment.

"You Can Provoke God To Act On Your Miracle Now!"

Hannah, in 1 Samuel 1, made a covenant with God, promising something in return for a miracle. She displayed unwavering faith by dedicating her son Samuel to the service of the Lord. It prompts us to ponder: What can we do for the Lord if He grants us what we currently lack? Have we identified any deficits in our community, and what steps have we taken to address them?

During my time in the House of Doom, I witnessed gifted talents snatched away by the devil through substance abuse. When I committed to faith, I told God that if He rescued me, I would work tirelessly to change the lives of addicts abandoned by society. God rescued me, and I turned down numerous job offers to remain unwavering in my commitment to this mission.

I am now prepared to spread the message of hope far and wide. He is capable of all things, for nothing is impossible if we wholeheartedly turn to Him (Jeremiah 32:25-26). I urge you to approach Him now; He is close at hand, eager to answer you promptly.

THE FOUR SURE STEPS

These four unerring steps serve as the paramount means to break free from the shackles of bondage. Applying them has liberated countless individuals trapped in despair and addiction.

The Math Analogy:
If you apply a specific formula to a complex problem and it consistently yields incorrect results, you must return to the drawing board. You cannot expect a different outcome using a flawed formula. You must adopt a new approach that actually produces results.

The antidote to alcoholism is complete cessation. It does not lie in merely reducing the quantity. The only definitive remedy lies in quitting and retracing your steps to the crossroads.

STEP 1: Return to the Crossroads (Jeremiah 6:16) Halt sinful behaviors and inquire about the tried-and-true path. This is the foundation for breaking free from suffering.
STEP 2: Repentance for Your Sins (Isaiah 1:18) God will extend complete forgiveness and erase your sins entirely, transforming you into a new creation.
STEP 3: Make Your Aspirations Known (Habakkuk 2:3) Cease relying solely on people. Cry out to God; He holds the answer when you seek Him with your whole heart.
STEP 4: Cultivate Possibility Faith (Mark 11:22-24) This is the faith that resides in your heart, is spoken with your mouth, and is received based on heartfelt belief.

The Thief (John 10:10)

Jesus enlightens us about the intentions of the thief: to steal, kill, and destroy. A thief targets what is precious—your computers, your smartphones, and your very soul.

How do I protect my family?

  • Choose friends carefully: Some are tools of the enemy disguised as sheep.
  • Educate your children: Talk openly about the dangers of drug abuse.
  • Monitor influences: Be careful with TV programs and unsupervised parties.
  • Build a foundation: Ensure your children have a strong start in the church.
